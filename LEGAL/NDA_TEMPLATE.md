## 1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 1.1 Confidential Information
For purposes of this Agreement, **"Confidential Information"** shall include, without limitation:

(a) Business plans, financial projections, and revenue models relating to the Project;
(b) Technical architecture, smart contract designs, blockchain specifications, and codebase;
(c) Token economics, distribution models, and vesting schedules;
(d) Go-to-market strategy, user acquisition plans, and marketing materials;
(e) Partnership strategies, investor lists, and funding approaches;
(f) Team composition, advisor relationships, and organizational structure;
(g) Intellectual property including patents, trademarks, trade secrets, and copyrights;
(h) Any other information clearly marked as "Confidential" or that, by its nature, would reasonably be considered confidential.

### 1.2 Exclusions from Confidential Information
Notwithstanding Section 1.1, the following shall **NOT** be considered Confidential Information:

(a) Information that is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;
(b) Information that was rightfully in the possession of the Receiving Party prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records dated before the disclosure date;
(c) Information that is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without any confidentiality obligation;
(d) Information that is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to the Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records;
(e) Information that must be disclosed by law, court order, or regulatory authority (see Section 4 for notification requirements).

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

### 2.1 Strict Confidentiality
The Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) Maintain the Confidential Information in strict confidence;
(b) Use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating the potential business relationship or investment opportunity;
(c) Limit disclosure to employees, advisors, attorneys, and consultants who have a legitimate "need to know" and who are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as this Agreement;
(d) Protect the Confidential Information using the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care;
(e) Not disclose the existence or substance of the Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.

### 2.2 Permitted Disclosure
The Receiving Party may disclose the Confidential Information only to:

(a) Its employees and contractors with a documented need to know;
(b) Its attorneys, accountants, and financial advisors (collectively, "Advisors");
(c) Potential investors or strategic partners (with Section 4 notification);
(d) Regulatory authorities (with Section 4 notification as permitted by law).

All such recipients must be informed of the confidential nature of the information and bound by written confidentiality obligations.

### 2.3 Return or Destruction
Upon request by the Disclosing Party, or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall:

(a) Return all Confidential Information in tangible form (documents, hardware, etc.); **OR**
(b) Certify in writing that all Confidential Information has been securely destroyed.

The Receiving Party may retain one archival copy for legal/regulatory compliance purposes, which shall remain subject to all confidentiality obligations.

## 3. RESTRICTIONS ON USE

### 3.1 No Rights Granted
Disclosure of Confidential Information does not grant the Receiving Party:

(a) Any license, right, or interest in the Confidential Information or any intellectual property;
(b) Any obligation by the Disclosing Party to enter into any business relationship or investment;
(c) Any right to modify, reproduce, or create derivative works;
(d) Any right to use the Disclosing Party's name or logo.

### 3.2 Non-Compete Acknowledgment
The Receiving Party acknowledges that:

(a) The Project is proprietary and highly competitive;
(b) Unauthorized use of the Confidential Information to develop a competing product would cause irreparable harm;
(c) The Receiving Party shall not, during the Term and for **[24/36/48]** months thereafter, use the Confidential Information to develop, market, or support any product or service directly competitive with the Project.

### 3.3 No Obligation to Proceed
The Disclosing Party shall have no obligation to:

(a) Explain or justify the Confidential Information;
(b) Provide additional information;
(c) Enter into any business relationship or investment.

The Receiving Party acknowledges that evaluation of the Confidential Information does not create any binding obligation on either party.

## 4. PERMITTED DISCLOSURE BY LAW

### 4.1 Legal Compulsion
If the Receiving Party is legally required to disclose Confidential Information by law, regulation, court order, subpoena, or governmental authority, the Receiving Party shall:

(a) Promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing (unless legally prohibited from doing so);
(b) Cooperate with the Disclosing Party in seeking protective orders or other remedies;
(c) Disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed;
(d) Request confidential treatment of the disclosed information.

### 4.2 No Waiver
Permitted disclosure under Section 4.1 shall not constitute a waiver of any confidentiality obligations.
